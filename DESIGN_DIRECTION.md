# Design & Copy Direction — Agreed in Discussion

## The Business
- **Name:** Cedar Dough Pizza Bar
- **Location:** Chatswood, Sydney (North Shore)
- **Type:** Levantine pizza bar / cafe — manakish, flatbreads, breakfast, all-day eats
- **Heritage:** Owner is Australian-born with Syrian roots, husband is Armenian
- **Logo:** Traditional Lebanese — cedar tree, Arabic text (عجينة الأرز), wheat stalks, green/gold
- **Interior:** Modern and clean — grey wood floors, white walls, charcoal accents, exposed brick counter, track lighting, green wall, marble tables, banquette seating
- **Food style:** Generous, warm, rustic — manakish, flatbreads, wraps, shakshuka, labneh, fresh veg, clay dishes

## Key Decisions

### Format
- Single HTML/CSS/JS page (no framework, no Next.js)
- All sections on one page with anchor navigation

### Target Market
- Chatswood, Sydney — North Shore
- Affluent professionals: Caucasian, Indian, Asian, some Middle Eastern
- Food-savvy, well-travelled, quality-conscious
- NOT a budget crowd — this is premium positioning

### Positioning
- Levantine pizza bar with premium presentation
- Heritage IS the brand (the name is "Cedar Dough" with Arabic) — don't hide it, just present it with sophistication
- Differentiated from generic brunch cafes AND cheap Middle Eastern takeaways
- The food is the hero — generous, real, photogenic

### Copy Approach
- Short, confident, editorial
- Don't over-explain heritage — the name and food speak for themselves
- Don't hype Jordan — za'atar is just one ingredient sourced from there, mention it as a small detail in story section only
- Avoid: "authentic", "traditional", over-explaining cultural background
- Hero headline direction: *Dough made fresh. Flavours made to share.*
- Subhead: Levantine pizza, breakfast, and all-day eats in the heart of Chatswood.

### Design Approach
- Light, warm, airy base (cream #F6F0E7) — not dark/moody
- One or two dark accent sections (deep brown #3D2C24) for rhythm
- Terracotta and olive as accent colors only
- Editorial serif headings + clean sans body
- Generous whitespace — restraint reads as confidence
- Smooth subtle transitions, no heavy animations
- Use real photos from the cafe (interior + food shots are strong)

### Single Page Structure
1. Nav — Logo left, minimal anchor links (Menu, Story, Visit)
2. Hero — Full-width, short headline, two CTAs
3. Story strip — One-liner philosophy + image
4. Specialties — 3-4 premium image cards (manakish, breakfast, flatbreads, sweets/coffee)
5. Menu highlights — Curated categories, not a full menu
6. Social proof — "Why people come back" row
7. Gallery — Tight grid, 6-8 images (food + interior)
8. Visit — Address, hours, map, CTAs
9. Footer — Instagram, contact, legal

### Hard Avoids
- No geometric arabesque patterns
- No "authentic"/"traditional" copy
- No food emojis or playful fonts
- No parallax scrolling or heavy animations
- No stock photography
- No generic cafe template feel
- No cheap Lebanese takeaway aesthetic
